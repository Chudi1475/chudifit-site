Why tall is different

Generic programs were never built for your leverage.

Most plans are written for a 5'9" body. At 6'2" and up the same lifts hit different. Deeper ranges of motion. More stress on your joints. And a frame that looks skinny until you put on real, structured size. Here are the four problems no template fixes:

Long levers make every rep harder.

Longer arms and legs mean the bar travels farther on every single rep. If your technique and exercise selection don't account for that, you end up grinding hard and growing slow.

Your joints take the hit.

Knees, shoulders, lower back, hips. A tall frame puts more torque on all of them. Bad programming doesn't just stall your progress, it breaks you down.

The "skinny-tall" math problem.

You need more muscle than a shorter guy just to look filled out. Random eating and random training will never cover that much real estate.

Nobody in your corner.

Willpower runs out. What actually changes your body is a coach who looks at your week, adjusts the plan, and holds you to it.
