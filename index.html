Why tall is different

Generic programs were never built for your leverage.

Cookie-cutter plans assume a 5'9" frame. At 6'2"+ the same lifts hit different — deeper ranges of motion, more load on your joints, and a body that looks "skinny" until you put on serious, structured size. Most tall guys spin their wheels for years because of four problems no template fixes:

Long levers, brutal leverage

Longer arms and legs mean every rep travels farther. Without height-specific technique and exercise selection, you grind hard and grow slow.

Joints that take the hit

Knees, shoulders, lower back, and hips carry more torque on a tall frame. Bad programming doesn't just stall you — it breaks you down.

"Skinny-tall" math

You need more muscle to look filled-out than a shorter guy does. Random eating and random training won't cover that much real estate.

No one in your corner

Willpower fades. What actually changes a tall athlete's body is a coach who adjusts the plan weekly and holds you to it.
